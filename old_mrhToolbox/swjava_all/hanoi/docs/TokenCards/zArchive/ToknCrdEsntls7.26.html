Getting Info and Getting Help
 

The Token Card is your key to authentication at Sun Microsystems. It calculates an alphanumeric password which grants you access for one login session.


Registering Your New Token Card

Note: You must change your Regis Password from it's original default to something else for you to be authenticated for any of these services.
  1. Visit the NetAdmin site.  Enter your Sun ID number and Regis Password. 

    2. http://netadmin
     
  2. Click on the link, "Token Card Administration".
  3. Click on the link, "Token Card Registration".
  4. Enter your Sun ID and Regis Password.
  5. Enter the Token Card Serial Number and select the submit button. 
  6. Enter the Token Card Serial Number again, for confirmation, and select the submit button.
  7. A confirmation will be displayed when the Token Card is activated. Please note your Login ID.
  8. You will need a PIN for your Token Card. Click on "Retrieve Your Token Card PIN".

Registering Your Replacement Token Card

  1. Visit the NetAdmin site.  Enter your Sun ID number and Regis Password. 

    2. http://netadmin
     
  2. Click on the link, "Token Card Administration".
  3. Click on the link, "Replacement Token Card Registration". 
  4. Enter your Sun ID and Regis Password.
  5. Enter the Replacement Token Card Serial Number and select the submit button. 
  6. Enter the Token Card Serial Number again, for confirmation, and select the submit button.
  7. A confirmation will be displayed when the Token Card is activated. Please note your Login ID.
  8. You will need a new PIN for your Token Card. Click on "Retrieve Your Token Card PIN".

Retrieving Your Token Card PIN

Before you connect for the first time, you must get your Token Card PIN. You can get your Token Card PIN online anytime 
  1. Visit the NetAdmin site.  Enter your Sun ID number and Regis Password. 

    2. http://netadmin
     
  2. Click on the link, "Token Card Administration".
  3. Click on the link, "Retrieve Your Token Card PIN". 
  4. Enter your Sun ID and Regis Password.
  5. Enter the Token Card Serial Number and select the submit button. 
  6. Your Token Card PIN is displayed.
If you have problems activating your Token Card and/or retrieving your PIN (or if you are a contractor or temp), please contact the nearest Resolution Center, or http://servicedesk/.

Changing Your Token Card PIN

  1. Press the Token Card's On button to turn on the card. The card's panel displays an EP prompt.
  2. Enter your 5-digit PIN. The card's panel displays an EH prompt.
  3. Press the Pin key. The card's panel displays an SP prompt.
  4. Enter your new 5-digit PIN. (Your new PIN must have 5 digits.) Your PIN has just changed!
    Note: If you enter your new PIN incorrectly, press the Clr key to cancel the new number. Your PIN reverts back to your pre-existing number.

Using Your Token Card

Please read all instructions before using your Token Card.
  • Your Token Card can be used for asynchronous challenge/response authentication, and synchronous password authentication.
  • Read all instructions before using your Token Card.
  • Please have your Token Card available before attempting to authenticate to Sun's network.
  • Keeping your Token Card PIN with your Token Card is a violation of Sun Security Policy.

Challenge/Response Authentication

  1. Once connected, you will be prompted for your Login ID. Your Login ID is your first initial, last initial, and SunID. Your first and last initials are based on your name entry in the HR database. If you are a contractor or temp, your Token ID is your first initial, last initial, the letter "c" or "t", and your SunID number. 
  2. The screen displays a challenge number. This challenge number appears in this format  9 #### (where #### is your 4-digit challenge)
  3. The screen will prompt you for a password. Each password is dynamically assigned using your Token Card, and is good for this session only. 
  4. To turn on the Token Card, press the ON button. The card's panel displays an EP prompt.
  5. Enter your 5-digit Token Card PIN. The card's panel displays an EH prompt.
  6. Enter the number nine (9). The card's panel displays a zero (0).
  7. Enter the 4-digit challenge (####) from the screen into the Token Card, and press the Entr key on the Token Card. Do not re-enter the preceding nine (9). The card's panel displays an alphanumeric password of 8 characters.
  8. On your terminal keyboard, enter the alphanumeric password that displays in the display panel of your Token Card, and press Return.   The password is NOT case-sensitive. The card's panel displays numbers zero (0) through nine (9) and letters A, C, E, F, H, and P. You must enter the password within 60 seconds after the terminal has displayed the challenge, or the connection will time out (you'll need to reconnect). 
      9. Caution: Be careful NOT to press the Pin key on the Token Card while logging in. If you press the Pin key on the card, you may change your Token Card PIN and lose your authentication ability. If you press the Pin key, the card's panel will display SP. Press the Clr key and then the ON button. Your PIN will not change.
  9. You are now connected to Sun Microsystems! 

Password Authentication (VPN Only)

  1. Once connected, you will be prompted for your password. Each password is dynamically assigned using your Token Card, and is good for this session only.
  2. To turn on the Token Card, press the ON button. The card's panel displays an EP prompt.
  3. Enter your 5-digit Token Card PIN. The card's panel displays an EH prompt.
  4. To generate a password, enter the number eight (8), and the Token Card will generate a password. An eight (8) will dance across the Token Card display, and an alphanumeric password will appear.
  5. On your terminal keyboard, enter the alphanumeric password that displays in the display panel of your Token Card, and press Return.   The password is NOT case-sensitive. The Token Card panel displays numbers zero (0) through nine (9) and letters A, C, E, F, H, and P. You must enter the password within 60 seconds after the terminal has prompted you for a password, or the connection will time out (you'll need to reconnect).

Lost/Stolen Token Cards

If your Token Card is lost or stolen, immediately submit a ServiceDesk ticket. A replacement Token Card will be distributed to you.
  1. Open Service Desk, and select "Report a Problem". 

    2. http://servicedesk/
     
  2. Select "Remote Connectivity".
  3. Select "Token Card Lost/Stolen", and fill out any comments relevant to the issue.

Token Card FAQs

Q: Why do vertical lines scroll across the display of the Token Card?
A: Entering your PIN incorrectly three consecutive times will cause the card to display scrolling lines. Leave the card on until it resets itself. Don't turn off the card until it has reset. The card's panel will display EP once it has reset. This can take as long as 10 minutes. After the card resets, enter your PIN. If the lines recur, submit a ServiceDesk request to obtain a replacement card.

Q: Why does the Token Card display "00000000"?
A: If the card displays eight 0's, the programming has been lost, and the card must be replaced.

Q: Why do I receive 2 four-digit challenges when I login, instead of the correct challenge of 9 ####?
A: Double-check that your Token Card ID was entered correctly. If it was entered correctly, place a ServiceDesk request to activate your card.

Q: Why does the terminal display "Attack lockout activated"?
A: After four consecutive wrong passwords, "Attack lockout activated" will display on your screen. You must wait 30 minutes for the attack lockout to clear. After 30 minutes, try logging in again.

Q: Why does the terminal display "Your record currently is in use"?
A: This message indicates a locking issue within the authentication database. Wait five minutes and then redial. The issue should be resolved.
