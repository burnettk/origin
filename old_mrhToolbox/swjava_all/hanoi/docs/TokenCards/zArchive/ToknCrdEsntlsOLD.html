The Token Card is your key to remote connectivity at Sun Microsystems. It is a password generator that you use each time you make a connection to a server that is considered security sensitive. It calculates an alphanumeric password which grants you access for that one login session.

Activating Your Token Card

Note: You must be on a Sun Microsystems (SWAN) site in order to access NetAdmin to activate your card and to use the online PIN (Personal Identification Number) retrieval application.

The Token Card you received may or may not be active depending on the services you have requested and what services you have access to. Regardless of whether it is or is not, we recommend that you attempt to activate it using the steps described below. If it is already active, you will receive a message informing you that it is already active. If it is not active, you will activate it yourself and receive a confirmation that you have done so.

To activate your Token Card:

  1. Visit the Token Card Administration site at:

    https://hanoi.central:443/servlet/TokenCardReg


  2. Enter your Sun ID number and Regis Password.
  3. Click on the link labeled Token Card Registration.
  4. Note your Login ID displayed on the page. You will need this to log in to Sun.Net.
  5. Enter the Token Card serial number found on the sticker on the back of the card and click on the Submit button.
  6. If your card has been successfully activated, you will receive a confirmation message. If your card has already been activated for you, you will receive the message, "Your card is already assigned to you or not in the database".
You now need to retieve your Token Card PIN prior to using your card.

Getting Your Token Card PIN

Before you connect to a Sun server for the first time, you must get your Token Card PIN. You can get your Token Card PIN online anytime (24 hours a day, 7 days a week) at:

https://hanoi.central:443/servlet/GetPin

To get your Token Card PIN, you need your:

  • Sun ID (Employee Number)
  • Regis Password
  • Token ID (your first initial, last initial, and Sun ID)
Example: Joe Nomad, Sun ID 98765, has a Token ID of: jn98765

Token Card Serial Number (the number on the back of the card)

To retrieve your Token Card PIN:

Click on the link labeled Retrieve Token Card PIN

Then, follow the instructions in the Token Card PIN application window.

Important Note: This service is currently not available to you if you do not have a Regis password (eg. if you are a Contractor or Temporary employee).

If you have problems retrieving your PIN (or you are a contractor or temp), please contact the nearest Resolution Center. Also, please see the section Getting Help and Information.

Using Your Token Card

Please read all instructions before using your Token Card

Please have your Token Card available before attempting to remotely connect to Sun's network

Keeping your Token Card PIN with your Token Card is a violation of Sun security

  1. Dial into your modempool (or ISP for Sun.Net).
  2. Once connected, you will be prompted for your Token Card ID (Sun.Net users, please see the section Sun.Net Access, at right, for URL information).

    Your Token ID is your first initial, last initial, and SunID. Your first and last initials are based on your name entry in the HR database. If you are a contractor or temp, your Token ID is your first initial, last initial, the letter "c" or "t", and your SunID number. (If you are using the Solaris PPP dialin window, the ID should be entered in the Token ID box prior to dialin.)

  3. The screen displays a challenge number. This challenge number appears in this format:

    9 #### (where #### is your four-digit challenge)

  4. The screen will prompt you for a password.

    Each password is dynamically assigned using your Token Card, and is good for this session only.

  5. To turn on the Token Card, press the ON button. The card's panel displays an EP prompt.
  6. Enter your 5-digit Token Card PIN. The card's panel displays an EH prompt.
  7. Enter the number nine (9). The card's panel displays a zero (0).
  8. Enter the 4-digit challenge (####) from the screen into the Token Card, and press the Entr key on the Token Card. Do not re-enter the preceding nine (9). The card's panel displays an alphanumeric password of 8 characters.

  9. On your terminal keyboard, enter the alphanumeric password that displays in the display panel of your Token Card, and press Return. The password is NOT case-sensitive. The card's panel displays numbers zero (0) through nine (9) and letters A, C, E, F, H, and P. You must enter the password within 30-45 seconds after the terminal has displayed the challenge, or the connection will timeout (you'll need to reconnect).

    Caution: Be careful NOT to press the Pin (P) key on the Token Card while logging in. If you press the Pin (P) key on the card, you may change your Token Card PIN and lose your authentication ability.

    If you press the Pin (P) key, the card's panel will display SP. Press the Clr (C) key and then the ON button. Your PIN will not change.

    If you do not press the Clr (C) key, but enter other numbers instead, your PIN will change to the numbers you just entered!

  10. You are now connected to Sun Microsystems!

Begin work, but remember you may see slower response times since you are working remotely.

Changing Your Token Card PIN

  1. Press the Token Card's On button to turn on the card. The card's panel displays an EP prompt.
  2. Enter your 5-digit PIN. The card's panel displays an EH prompt.
  3. Press the Pin (P) key. The card's panel displays an SP prompt.
  4. Enter your new 5-digit PIN. (Your new PIN must have 5 digits.) Your PIN has just changed! Note: If you enter your new PIN incorrectly, press the Clr key to cancel the new number. Your PIN reverts back to your pre-existing number.

Token Card FAQs

Q: Why do vertical lines scroll across the display of the Token Card?
A: Entering your PIN incorrectly three consecutive times will cause the card to display scrolling lines. Leave the card on until it resets itself. Don't turn off the card until it has reset. The card's panel will display EP once it has reset. This can take as long as 10 minutes. After the card resets, enter your PIN. If the lines recur, submit a ServiceDesk request to obtain a replacement card.

Q: Why does the Token Card display "00000000"?
A: If the card displays eight 0's, the programming has been lost, and the card will need to be replaced.

Q: Why do I receive 2 four-digit challenges when I login, instead of the correct challenge of 9 ####?
A: Double-check that your Token Card ID was entered correctly. If it was entered correctly, your card has not been activated correctly in the server database. Place a ServiceDesk request to activate your card.

Q: Why does the terminal display "Attack lockout activated"?
A: After four consecutive wrong passwords, "Attack lockout activated" will display on your screen. You must wait 30 minutes for the attack lockout to clear. After 30 minutes, try logging in again.

Q:Why does the terminal display "Your record currently is in use"?
A: This message indicates a locking issue within the authentication database. Wait five minutes and then redial. The issue should be resolved.
